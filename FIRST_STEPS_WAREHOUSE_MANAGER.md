## Phase 3: Goods Receiving Process (Days 2-5)

### Day 2-3: Understanding Purchase Orders
**Estimated Time:** 2-3 hours

1. **Navigate to Purchase Orders**
   - Review upcoming deliveries
   - Check PO details:
     - Supplier name
     - Expected delivery date
     - Products ordered with quantities
     - Total value
   - Note what to expect this week

2. **Prepare for Receiving**
   - Clear receiving dock space
   - Charge barcode scanners
   - Prepare inspection tools:
     - Camera/tablet for photos
     - Measuring tape (for dimensions/weights)
     - Quality checklist
   - Review product specifications

### Day 3-4: Create Your First GRN
**Estimated Time:** 2-4 hours (depending on shipment size)

1. **When Shipment Arrives**
   - Verify supplier delivery note matches PO
   - Count packages/pallets
   - Check for obvious external damage
   - Sign delivery receipt

2. **Navigate to Receiving â†’ Create GRN**
   - Click "Create GRN"
   - Select Purchase Order (system shows expected items)
   - Begin receiving process

3. **Receive Items (Barcode Scanning Method)**
   - Scan product barcode
   - Enter quantity received
   - System compares to PO:
     - âœ… Match: Green indicator
     - âš ï¸ Variance: Yellow/red warning
   - Repeat for all products

4. **Document Variances**
   If quantities don't match:
   - **Shortage:** Received less than ordered
     - Enter actual quantity received
     - Add note: "Short 5 units, supplier to credit"
     - Take photo of delivery note
   
   - **Overage:** Received more than ordered
     - Enter actual quantity received
     - Add note: "Extra 3 units, will return or accept"
     - Verify with Super Manager
   
   - **Damage:** Items damaged in transit
     - Enter quantity damaged
     - Take photos of damage (multiple angles)
     - Add note: "Damaged in transit, claim with supplier"
     - Set aside damaged units

5. **Quality Inspection**
   - Randomly inspect 10% of items (or all for high-value)
   - Check for:
     - Correct product (matches SKU)
     - Correct specifications (size, color, version)
     - No defects or damage
     - Packaging intact
     - Expiry dates (if applicable)
   - Document findings with photos

6. **Save GRN**
   - Click "Save GRN"
   - Status: "Draft" or "Pending Acceptance"
   - âš ï¸ **IMPORTANT:** Inventory is NOT updated yet

## Phase 4: Stock Transfer Fulfillment (Days 7-14)

### Day 7-8: Understanding Transfer Requests
**Estimated Time:** 2-3 hours

1. **Navigate to Stock Transfers**
   - Review pending transfer requests
   - Understand request details:
     - Requesting outlet
     - Products needed
     - Quantities requested
     - Urgency level
     - Reason for transfer

2. **Check Stock Availability**
   - Verify warehouse has sufficient stock
   - Check reserved stock (already committed)
   - Note if items are in multiple locations

3. **Understand Transfer Workflow**
   - **Step 1:** Outlet creates transfer request
   - **Step 2:** Super Manager approves request
   - **Step 3:** You (Warehouse Manager) fulfill transfer:
     - Pick items
     - Pack items
     - Mark "In Transit"
   - **Step 4:** Receiving outlet confirms receipt and completes transfer

### Day 9-11: Fulfill Your First Transfer
**Estimated Time:** 2-4 hours

1. **Pick Items**
   - Print picking list (or use mobile device)
   - Navigate warehouse to product locations
   - Scan barcode to verify correct item
   - Count quantities carefully
   - Double-check against picking list

2. **Quality Check**
   - Inspect items before packing
   - Ensure:
     - Correct products
     - Good condition (no damage)
     - Proper quantities
     - Expiry dates (if applicable)

3. **Pack Items**
   - Use appropriate packaging
   - Protect fragile items
   - Label boxes with:
     - Destination outlet
     - Product contents
     - Quantity
     - Your initials/date
   - Attach transfer reference number

4. **Mark "In Transit"**
   - Navigate to Stock Transfers
   - Open transfer request
   - Click "Mark In Transit"
   - âœ… **AUTOMATIC:** Stock is deducted from warehouse immediately
   - Enter:
     - Tracking number (if using courier)
     - Estimated arrival date
     - Number of packages
     - Notes for receiving outlet

5. **Arrange Transportation**
   - **Direct Delivery:** Use company vehicle
   - **Courier:** Book courier pickup
   - **Customer Pickup:** Notify outlet when ready
   - Update tracking information in system

6. **Follow Up**
   - Monitor transfer status
   - Chase up if not received within expected time
   - Be available for receiving outlet questions

### Day 12-14: Handle Transfer Variations
**Estimated Time:** 1-2 hours

1. **Partial Fulfillment**
   - If warehouse doesn't have full quantity:
     - Fulfill what's available
     - Create backorder for remaining
     - Notify requesting outlet
     - Update transfer with partial quantities

2. **Substitutions**
   - If product unavailable:
     - Contact requesting outlet
     - Suggest substitute product
     - Get approval before sending
     - Document substitution in transfer notes

3. **Emergency Transfers**
   - High-priority transfers (stock-out at outlet):
     - Prioritize picking and packing
     - Use fastest shipping method
     - Notify outlet of dispatch time
     - Track delivery status

## Phase 5: Stock Audit & Cycle Counting (Days 14-21)

### Day 14-15: Understanding Stock Audits
**Estimated Time:** 2-3 hours

1. **Navigate to Stock Audit â†’ Audit Dashboard**
   - Review audit types:
     - **Full Audit:** Count entire warehouse (annual)
     - **Cycle Count:** Count specific categories (monthly)
     - **Spot Check:** Random checks (daily/weekly)
   - Understand audit workflow:
     - Create audit
     - Count products
     - Submit counts
     - System compares to recorded inventory
     - Investigate variances
     - Approve adjustments

2. **Review Previous Audits**
   - Check historical audit results
   - Identify problem areas:
     - Products with frequent variances
     - Categories with accuracy issues
   - Learn from past mistakes

### Day 16-18: Conduct Your First Cycle Count
**Estimated Time:** 3-5 hours (depending on size)

1. **Create Cycle Count**
   - Navigate to Stock Audit â†’ Create Audit
   - Select "Cycle Count"
   - Choose category or specific products
   - Assign to yourself
   - Set count date
   - System generates count sheet

2. **Prepare for Count**
   - Schedule during low-activity time
   - Pause stock movements in count area (if possible)
   - Print count sheets or use mobile device
   - Get barcode scanner
   - Bring pen/paper for notes

3. **Perform Physical Count**
   - Go to physical location
   - Scan barcode or enter SKU
   - Count units carefully:
     - Open boxes to count individual units
     - Check behind/above for hidden stock
     - Count damaged units separately
   - Enter actual count in system
   - Add notes for anything unusual

4. **Submit Count**
   - Review all counts before submitting
   - Double-check counts for high-value items
   - Click "Submit Count"
   - System generates variance report

### Day 19-20: Variance Investigation
**Estimated Time:** 2-4 hours

1. **Review Variance Report**
   - System shows discrepancies:
     - Expected quantity (system)
     - Actual quantity (your count)
     - Variance (difference)
     - Variance % (percentage difference)
     - Value impact (financial)

2. **Variance Severity Levels**
   - ðŸŸ¢ **Minor:** <2% variance, low value
   - ðŸŸ¡ **Moderate:** 2-5% variance or medium value
   - ðŸ”´ **Critical:** >5% variance or high value

3. **Investigate Variances**
   For each variance:
   - **Check recent movements:**
     - Were items received recently?
     - Were transfers sent out?
     - Were there sales/orders?
     - Was there a production batch?
   
   - **Physical recount:**
     - Count again to confirm
     - Check alternate locations
     - Look for mislabeled items
   
   - **Review documentation:**
     - Check if GRN was accepted
     - Verify transfer was marked "In Transit"
     - Review recent adjustments
   
   - **Document findings:**
     - Add notes to variance report
     - Upload photos if needed
     - Explain likely cause

4. **Common Variance Causes**
   - GRN saved but not accepted (inventory not updated)
   - Transfer marked "In Transit" (stock already deducted)
   - Theft or damage not documented
   - Data entry errors
   - Products stored in wrong location
   - Barcode scanning errors

## Pro Tips for Success

### Receiving Accuracy
- **Count twice for high-value items:** Accuracy is critical
- **Photograph everything:** Evidence for disputes
- **Document immediately:** Don't rely on memory
- **Communicate with suppliers:** Build relationships for faster issue resolution

### Transfer Efficiency
- **Batch transfers:** Fulfill multiple transfers to same outlet together
- **Optimize picking routes:** Reduce walking time
- **Use proper packaging:** Prevent damage in transit
- **Track performance:** Monitor your average fulfillment time

### Inventory Accuracy
- **FIFO rotation:** First in, first out prevents expiry
- **Proper labeling:** Clear labels prevent picking errors
- **Regular cycle counts:** Small frequent counts > large annual audits
- **Root cause analysis:** Don't just fix variances, understand why they happened

### Barcode Scanning
- **Always scan:** Don't rely on visual identification
- **Clean scanners:** Dirty scanners cause errors
- **Verify beep:** Ensure scan registered before moving on
- **Manual entry backup:** Have process if scanner fails

## Common Challenges & Solutions

### Challenge: Large GRN variances
**Solution:**
- Recount immediately before supplier leaves
- Document with photos and video
- Get delivery driver signature on variance note
- Contact supplier same day

### Challenge: Transfer fulfillment delays
**Solution:**
- Prioritize emergency transfers
- Batch non-urgent transfers
- Improve picking route optimization
- Add temporary staff during busy periods

### Challenge: Recurring inventory discrepancies
**Solution:**
- Increase cycle count frequency for problem items
- Review staff training on processes
- Check for security issues (theft)
- Verify all GRNs are being accepted (not just saved)

### Challenge: Warehouse disorganization
**Solution:**
- Implement bin location system
- Use ABC analysis (A=high-value, B=medium, C=low)
- Store fast-moving items near dispatch area
- Regular cleanup and reorganization days
