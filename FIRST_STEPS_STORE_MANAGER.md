## Phase 3: Point of Sale (POS) Setup (Days 2-5)

### Day 2-3: POS Session Management
**Estimated Time:** 2-3 hours

1. **Understand POS Sessions**
   - Navigate to POS â†’ POS Main
   - **POS Session:** A shift where you track sales
   - Must open session before selling
   - Must close session at end of shift
   - Cash reconciliation at session close

2. **Open Your First POS Session**
   - Click "Open Session"
   - Enter:
     - Session name (e.g., "Morning Shift - 2025-10-29")
     - Opening cash amount (starting cash in register)
     - Your name as operator
   - Click "Start Session"

3. **Navigate POS Interface**
   - **Left Side:** Product selector

     - Scan barcode
     - Browse categories

### Day 3-5: Process Your First Sales
**Estimated Time:** 3-5 hours of practice

1. **Create Simple Sale**
   - Open POS session
   - Search for product or scan barcode
   - Product appears in cart
   - Adjust quantity if needed
   - Click "Payment"
   - Select payment method:
     - Cash
     - Card
     - Mobile payment
   - Enter amount received (for cash)
   - System shows change due
   - Click "Complete Sale"
   - âœ… **AUTOMATIC:** Inventory is deducted immediately
   - Print receipt for customer

2. **Practice Multiple Items**
   - Add multiple products to cart
   - Adjust quantities
   - Apply discounts (if authorized)
   - Remove items if needed
   - Process payment
   - Verify inventory deducted correctly

3. **Handle Split Payments**
   - Customer pays partly cash, partly card
   - Add first payment method and amount
   - Add second payment method and amount
   - Complete sale

4. **Park and Resume Sales**
   - "Park Sale" for customer who needs to get more items
   - Serve another customer
   - Resume parked sale later
   - Complete original sale

5. **Void/Cancel Sales**
   - If sale needs cancellation before payment:
     - Clear cart
   - If sale completed but needs refund:
     - Create return (covered in Phase 5)

**Estimated Time:** 2-3 hours

   - **Step 5:** Refund processed

3. **Review Pending Returns**

   - Note customer names and items

### Day 16-18: Process Your First Return
**Estimated Time:** 2-3 hours

1. **Customer Brings Return**
   - Verify return authorization
   - Check customer ID matches order
   - Navigate to Returns â†’ Find Return Request

2. **Inspect Returned Item**
   - Check condition:
     - âœ… **Resaleable:** New condition, tags intact

3. **Mark Return as Received**
   - Open return request
   - Click "Mark Received"
   - Select condition:
     - Resaleable
     - Minor damage (partial refund)
     - Major damage (no refund)

     - Your outlet (if resaleable)

   - System calculates refund amount:
     - Full refund: Resaleable condition
     - Partial refund: Minor damage
     - No refund: Customer misuse
   - Select refund method:
     - Cash (if original payment was cash)
     - Original payment method
     - Store credit
   - Give receipt to customer
   - Refund processed by finance team

### Day 19-21: Handle Complex Returns
**Estimated Time:** 2-3 hours

1. **Returns Without Receipt**
   - Search order by:
     - Customer phone number
     - Customer email
     - Order number
   - Verify purchase in system
   - Process as normal if found
   - Offer store credit if not found

2. **Damaged in Transit**
   - Take detailed photos
   - Document damage
   - Full refund to customer
   - File claim with courier
   - Do not restock damaged item

3. **Wrong Item Sent**
   - Apologize to customer
   - Process return for wrong item
   - Create new order for correct item
   - Expedite shipping
   - Waive shipping charges

4. **Suspected Wardrobing** (Customer used item, then returned)
   - Document condition with photos
   - Note signs of use:
     - Missing tags
     - Wear marks
     - Odors
     - Damage
   - Escalate to Super Manager
   - Partial or no refund based on policy
   - Flag customer if pattern emerges

## Pro Tips for Success

### POS Efficiency
- **Practice scanning:** Fast scanning improves customer experience
- **Memorize top items:** Know bestsellers by SKU

- **Be accurate:** Speed without accuracy causes problems

### Inventory Management
- **Check daily:** Review low stock alerts every morning
- **Request proactively:** Don't wait for stock-outs
- **FIFO rotation:** Sell oldest stock first

### Customer Service
- **Know your products:** Understand features and benefits
- **Be helpful:** Guide customers to right products
- **Follow up:** Contact customers about their orders
- **Handle complaints gracefully:** Empathy goes a long way

### Returns Handling
- **Be thorough:** Inspect returns carefully
- **Document everything:** Photos protect against disputes
- **Be fair:** Follow policy consistently
- **Watch for patterns:** Flag repeat returners

## Common Challenges & Solutions

### Challenge: POS system slow during peak hours
**Solution:**
- Close unnecessary applications
- Clear browser cache
- Restart POS at low-traffic time
- Report persistent issues to IT

### Challenge: Customer wants item you don't have
**Solution:**
- Check stock at other outlets
- Create transfer request
- Offer to ship when available
- Suggest similar alternatives

### Challenge: Stock levels don't match physical count
**Solution:**
- Perform spot count immediately
- Check recent transactions
- Verify all sales were recorded
- Report to Super Manager for adjustment

### Challenge: Difficult customer return
**Solution:**
- Stay calm and professional
- Review return policy together
- Document condition thoroughly
- Escalate to Super Manager if needed
